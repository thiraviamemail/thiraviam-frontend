Welcome to the Solar System

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It has a rocky surface and extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, known for its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide and surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has abundant water, a protective atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, often called the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It is a major focus of exploration for human colonization.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with a Great Red Spot storm and dozens of moons including Ganymede and Europa.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles. It is a gas giant with many moons, including Titan.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, making its seasons very unusual.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun, a deep blue ice giant with strong winds and storms. It has moons such as Triton.
